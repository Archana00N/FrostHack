The Problem

  • There is an earnest problem of availibility of oxygen for patients that are severely affected due to the virus.
  • With the availability of healthcare facilities decreasing drastically, the struggle to find oxygen is getting worse.
In our country, there is a shortage of cryogenic tankers, i.e. the infamous "Pill shaped liquid oxygen carrying trucks".
It carries, stores and transports liquid gases at sub-zero temperatures. Manufacturing of such vessels requires special technical know how and sophisticated fabrication techniques.
Hence, our country has also seen a few mishaps regarding those tankers.

Oxygen Services

The Problem

Masks protect us against SARS-CoV-2, the coronavirus that causes Covid-19. Vaccine protects against the severity of Covid-19. And, oxygen gives extra hours and days to patients for medicines to alleviate symptoms.

The oxygen that a Covid-19 patient requires is medical oxygen. Medical oxygen is produced in specialised manufacturing units as industrial oxygen and purified over 93 per cent for the use of patients suffering from oxygen starvation.

In the cases of Covid-19, severely ill patients need oxygen support as they fail to utilise environmental oxygen in their respiration.

Oxygen needs of Covid-19 patients vary depending on the severity of their illness. On an average, less than 10 per cent Covid-19 patients need oxygen support. Fewer number of Covid-19 patients require usage of high flow nasal cannula (HFNC).

The Solution
